Jordan - Take Flight

Interactive installation • Kinect • 3D Rendering

Jordan Hangar is designed to be a offseason training home with local high school games and product testings + releases.

Jordan Brand was interested in bringing interactive experience into their new "Jordan Hangar" in Los Angles. They came to AKQA to initiate a one-week "hackathon" which we create lots of concepts and functional prototypes. We moved forward with one of the ideas, and combined some other ideas into this interactive training experience - "The Cube", along with an engaging user journey inside the Jordan Hangar.

Client: Nike Jordan

Agency: AKQA

Role: Digital Prototyping, User Experience, 3D Rendering



OUR MISSION

"Create an interactive training experience in the Jordan Hangar that embodies the key features of Jordan Brand - Simple, Soulful, Fearless and Epic."







DESIGN PROCESS

Brand Study

In order to create an experience that matches Jordan brand, we studied how people perceive Jordan, and how Jordan position itself as a Brand. At first we thought of Jordan as a brand of elite, performance, and athleticism, but there’s more about it. Jordan brand embraces the idea of family, for example Chris Paul, Blake Griffin are part of the Jordan family, while the young talents are the “Nephews” of Jordan. These nephews want to elevate and evolve with Jordan, they want to learn from the best and be the best. So how can we create an experience that supports the idea of family, encourages the young talents, and also celebrates the love of the game?










Concepting + Prototyping

During the ideation process, my teammate Steven Phan and I came up with an interactive training station that can gather your performance data to an online profile, where people can access the information and furthermore get selected to the Jordan Brand Classic game, the game where they can have a high chance to get recognition by the professionals. We also prototyped a training game using Kinect to track your body movement and test your quickness and dribbling skill.

FINAL PRODUCT

After the one-week hackathon, our idea got selected to move forward, so we teamed up with more people to help combine the ideas. We started by thinking about the whole user flow in the Jordan Hangar, and how our training station can play a part in the experience. We transformed our training station into a cube where four people can train at the same time, we called it the “Flight Cell”. We also integrated an RFID wristband as a personal ID that can help you collect all the data during your time in the Jordan Hangar. With a custom app you can enter your ID number to access all your information, and share your status with friends.





















Training Experience + Drills

In addition to designing the whole user flow in the space, we also dived into the actual training experience, figuring out what types of drills we want to provide. We wanted this experience to be more than just a fun game to play with, so we consulted a personal trainer to refine the drills and eventually customized two sets of training sessions that were inspired by Chris Paul and Blake Griffin.













EPILOGUE

Our idea was eventually adapted by Jordan but not with the full experience due to the time constraint. They built the Cube with different training videos playing on the screens. People can jump in and start following the person on the screen, and try different types of dribbling. They also adapted the wristband idea into the All Star Weekend, which helped integrate a holistic and personal experience for the users. Even though we didn’t help build the cube, it was still a great experience working on such important project as an intern at AKQA, and we were very content that Jordan loved our idea and wanted to bring it to more places.
