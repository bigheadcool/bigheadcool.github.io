Google Glass Development

Glassware Development • Video Editing • UX / UI

We developed several Glass Apps to showcase the usage of Google Glass in different scenarios such as hiking and cooking.

Google Glass team came to AKQA last year, wanted us to come up with ideas for Google Glass Apps (Glasswares) for their new version of Google Glass. We had a rapid one-week process concepting and prototyping ideas for the Google Glass team, and picked 3 ideas to actually develop on Google Glass for one month.

Client: Google Glass

Agency: AKQA

Role: Glassware Development • Video Editing • UX / UI



OUR MISSION

"Create a proof-of-concept of a Glassware app, and tell a great story as to why people would want to have it in their daily lives."







DESIGN PROCESS

Brand Study

Although Google Glass is born of Google, it has some very distinct attributes, such as elegant, sophisticated, lifestyle, and soft colors, while some are overlap with Google, such as simplicity, user focused, technology, and innovation. Google glass doesn’t want to interrupt people’s lives, it wants to let people do what they do best, and let technology do what it does the best. It’s about being present, hands free and eyes open through Google glass to engage with people, and embrace the moment you love. So how can we leverage what Google Glass does best, while enhancing people’s daily lives?










Concept Video

Google glass did a pretty good job at navigation, exploration, capturing and sharing, so my team focused on that and decided to create an app for travel purpose. We came up with a time-base tour recommendation app called “Local Scene”, which lets you explore a new city or even a place you are already familiar with base on your preferences. We not only made a concept video showing how it could work, but also developed an App in two days showing the user flow using actual data from Maps API.













FINAL PRODUCT

After the one-week hackathon, we had 5 solid ideas for Google Glass team and they decided to let us continue exploring application around cooking, hiking, and how-to. We had a new team of nine developers working together on different parts of the apps, and eventually delivered the three apps to them in one and a half month. Here a sneak peak of the hiking app: There are two main functions, explore nearby trail and track my own route. There are also additional functions such as sunset alert and compass view.

User Flow

Here an overview of the user flow, users are able to use voice command to either explore nearby trails or track a route. From nearby trail you can follow the navigation, check the maps, and also see photos about the places. If you track your route, you can add a waypoint at a certain location of your trip, add a note or add a photo about this location, and then access these information afterwards. We wanted to create something that are intuitive to command, that can facilitate the exploration process, and also help relive your experience about certain locations.












EPILOGUE

For this project, I was mainly working in Android Studio, figuring out the user flow, and coding the structure of the app and the layout of the screens. At the same time I was also working closely with quality assurance engineers to fix the bugs. Even though Google decided to put the Google Glass Explorer program on hold, I’m still glad I went though this process, learning a lot about Gitflow workflow and how to work as a developer in a team. Hopefully we can jump back to develop Google Glass again when the new generation comes out.
